About

About This Project / عن المشروع 🌿

Cinematic Counter-Cartographies is not just an academic archive. This is a living, growing project for anyone who cares about Palestinian stories, memory, and resistance. I am proud to be part of a scholarly community, but this work is for my people first — that is why you will find much of the site in Arabic too.

If you are Palestinian, Arab, or just curious, this space is for you. Your voice matters here — you can add your own stories, films, or checkpoint experiences directly to the archive through the map page.

The Argument

Palestinian women filmmakers do not simply document occupation — they devise formal strategies that refuse it. This archive maps how Mai Masri, Annemarie Jacir, and Farah Nabulsi transform checkpoints, carceral spaces, and surveillance infrastructures into sites of cinematic resistance across twenty-five years of Palestinian film.

Mai Masri

3000 Nights

Imprisoned on false charges, a young Palestinian schoolteacher gives birth to her son in an Israeli prison. As she navigates the carceral system, her story becomes one of resistance, solidarity, and survival under occupation.
